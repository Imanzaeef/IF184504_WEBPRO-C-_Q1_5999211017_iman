SUMMARY


Penang is a Malaysian state located on the northwest coast of Peninsular Malaysia, by the Malacca Strait. It has two parts: Penang Island, where the capital city, George Town, is located, and Seberang Perai (Mainland) on the Malay Peninsula. They are connected by Malaysia's two longest road bridges, the Penang Bridge and the Sultan Abdul Halim Muadzam Shah Bridge (Penang Bridge 2); the latter is also, as of May 2019, the longest oversea bridge in Southeast Asia. The second smallest Malaysian state by land mass after Perlis on the north, Penang is bordered by Kedah to the north and the east, and Perak to the south.



Through the census, the population of Penang amounted to almost 1.767 million in 2018, while its population density increased to 1,684 / km2 (4,360 / sq km). It has the highest population density in the country and is one of the most urban cities after Kuala Lumpur, the capital of Malaysia. Seberang Peraiis the second largest city in Malaysia by population. Its heterogeneous population is very different in terms of ethnicity, culture, language and religion. Apart from the three main races, the Malays, Chinese, and Indians, my hometown is also home to the Eurasian, Siamese and expatriate communities. George Town is my captal hometown is also home to a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The people here often name the people of Penang as Penangite. The modern history of Penang began in 1786, after the founding of George Town by Francis Light. Penang formed part of the Straits Settlements in 1826, which became a British crown colony in 1867. Direct British rule was only briefly interrupted during World War II, when the Japanese occupied Penang; The British took back Penang in 1945. Penang was later merged with the Federation of Malaya (now Malaysia), which gained independence from the British in 1957. Following the decline in its enterprise trade in the 1970s, Penang’s economy was geared towards high-tech manufacturing.


PICTURES OF MY HOMETOWN


Kompleks Tun Abdul Razak (KOMTAR)

In Penang city, Malaysia, KOMTAR Tower is the highest skyscraper in the State and Malaysia's tenth highest building. KOMTAR is an abbreviation for the second Malaysian Prime Minister, Kompleks Tun Abdul Razak. It was built in 1974 and finished in 1986. When the skycraper finished in 1985, 65 storeys at a height of 232 metres were initially completed (761 ft). The KOMTAR Tower was finished after Sunshine 60 in Tokyo and was Asia's second highest structure. For three more years the building remained the highest skyscraper in Malaysia, till Menara Maybank in Kuala Lumpur in 1988 surpassed it. KOMTAR Tower remains, to this day, uncontested as Penang's highest building.



Georgetown

The Malaysian state of Penang capital is George Town. The city is the third most populated city in Malaysia with a population of 708127 since 2010, but the Greater Penang is the second biggest city in the country after the Greater Kuala Lumpur, which has a population of 2,412,616 residents in 2010. Since 2008, the historic centre of George Town has become a UNESCO World Heritage Site. George Town was the first British colony in Southeast Asia, established up as a storehouse of the Francis Light of East India Company in 1786. George Town was part of the Straits Settlements, together with Singapore and Malacca, which, in the year 1867, became a British Crown Colony. During the Second World Conflict it was subject to Japan before the British caught it again at the end of the war. Shortly before Malaya became independent of the British in 1957, it was proclaimed Queen Elizabeth II the city and became the first city in modern history. George Town.



Penang Bridge

The The Penang Bridge is located in Penang, Malaysia, on a 13.5-kilometer (8.4 kilometre) dual carriageway pier. On the mainland side of the state the bridge connects Perai with Gelugor on the island over the Strait of Penang. The first and only road connecting the peninsula to the island was indeed the bridge until 2014. The bridge, with an overall length of 8.4 km across water, is the second-last bridge in Malaysia and the fifth longest in South East Asia (5.2 miles). On 14 September 1985, the bridge was opened. PLUS Expressways is the incumbent bridge holder and maintenance. The concession holder was Penang Bridge Sdn Bhd until it was amalgamated with the current dealer.
