SUMMARY


The Malaysian state of Penang comprises of a peninsula and an island which are linked by two of the country's longest bridges. Because of its colonial architecture, connected with the 2nd World War and the various green areas that encompass anything from formal botanical gardens to tropical jungle, George Town, Penang's capital, has become a significant tourist attraction in Malaysia. George Town sections were also declared UNESCO World Heritage Sites, partly due to their marine urban communities known as clan jetties. See my list of Penang's best tourist attractions for suggestions on how to spend your time after you reach Malaysia.


BEST TOURIST ATTRACTION PLACE TO VISIT


PENANG HILL

The Penang Hill, which has been built as the first colonial hill station in Malaysia, has a rich and colourful history, which is absolutely a must see when staying in Penang. Penang Hill is also a local place known as Bukit Bendara. Penang Hill is located 821 metres above Georgetown and provides fantastic views of the city from the top as well as a cold environment, roughly 5 degrees cooler than the lowlands. Due to its amazing vistas and cool, unpolluted air, this is a favourite resort for local residents and tourists alike!



ESCAPE PARK PENANG

Penang Escape Park is surrounded by greenery and consists of 2 main sections – adventure and water play in the middle of nature, which was formerly a 44-acre wasteland. Spend your day in the most thrilling amusement park in Penang and try out with family and friends over 38 rides and attractions. The park provides a view over the amazing natural environment of Malaysia, which is the perfect spot to relax and have fun for both your family and you. All rides in the park are well adapted to individuals of every age and energy – the various attractions in the theme park guarantee a lot of fun for both adults and children. ESCAPE Penang would be a great day for the entire family, from the obstacle courses to the diving towers! The theme park contains the Guinness World Record for the world's longest tube water slide. The one-of-a-kind slide is 1111m long and welcomes everyone looking for a taste of adrenaline thrill.



BATU FERRINGHI

The suburb of George Town in Penang, Malaysia, is Batu Ferringhii. Located in the north shore of Penang Island, it is the primary beach destination for locals and visitors, approximately 11 kilometres (6.8 mi) north-west of the city centre. A number of important high-rise hotels were constructed along the 4-kilometer stretch of beaches, notably the Hard Rock Hotel, in order to cope with the inflow of guests. There are also several water sports at beach resorts along Batu Ferringhi, including parasailing. On a clear day, a magnificent view of the Andaman Sea and Mount Jerai, situated in Kedah, was possible. In addition, Batu Ferringhi is famous for its night market that offers a wide variety of merchandise and street food.
